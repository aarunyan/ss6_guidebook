page11 n-gram demo
===================
Training sentences:
  - I eat rice
  - I eat noodles
  - I eat rice
  - You eat rice
  - We eat rice
  - They eat noodles
  - Cats chase mice
  - Dogs chase cats

Key bigram counts:
  Count("i eat") = 3
  Count("eat rice") = 4
  Count("eat noodles") = 2
  Count("chase cats") = 1

Bigram formula: P(next | previous) = Count(previous, next) / Count(previous)
  Count("eat") = 6
  P("rice" | "eat") = 4/6 = 0.67
  P("noodles" | "eat") = 2/6 = 0.33

Runtime view for context "eat":
   eat -> rice     #############        P=0.67 count=4
   eat -> noodles  ######               P=0.33 count=2

What the demo shows:
  1. Short context: the model only looks one word back in a bigram model.
  2. Vocabulary explosion: many possible word pairs are never observed.
  3. No meaning: "eat" is predicted from counts, not from understanding.
